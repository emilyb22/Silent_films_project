Music

Although the projected images themselves were soundless, the production of most silent films were never actually silent. Music ranged widely in the way it accompanied silent film. Some exhibitors would play songs through phonographs with the addition of specialized equipment for sound effects.

Depending on where the films were being screened, shows included piano players, organists, drummers and even full orchestras. Higher end silent films were known to have their own musical directors that would orchestrate pieces to play along with the film and in between reels.This music was either scored for screening itself, or taken from classical pieces.

The idea was that the music would add to the experience of the show, it would heighten emotions in the audience and intensify the complexity of the storyline. Exhibitors would even have comics come on stage and engage with the crowd while encouraging patrons to sing along as song lyrics flashed on the screen. The addition of music added to the social aspect that popularized silent films as a form of entertainment.
