Top Films

Projection speed

The first silent films were shot and played at variable speeds, or frame rates, of between 12 and 26 frames per second (fps). At the time, the cameramen operated the projectors claimed that the standard silent film projection speed was 16 fps. Also, projectionists would often receive instructions for silent films with cue sheets that would tell them at which points in the film they were to crank faster or slower.

Popular films

The most popular film styles for silent film area were comedy and adventure, less favoured were films of contemporary settings. The Birth of a Nation was assumed to dominate box office successes in terms of top films in the era, however there were many other pictures between 1922 and 1927 that took rise above the rest. These included Robin Hood, The Big Parade, The Covered Wagon and Ben-Hur.
